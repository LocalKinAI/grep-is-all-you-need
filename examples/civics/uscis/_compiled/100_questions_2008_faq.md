# 100_questions_2008.txt — FAQ

## Q1: How many civics questions are asked during the naturalization interview, and what is the passing score?
A: A USCIS Officer will ask up to 10 questions from the official pool of 100. You must answer 6 out of the 10 questions correctly to pass the civics portion. The test is administered orally. (Source: 100_questions_2008.txt)

## Q2: Are there study exceptions for older or long-term permanent residents?
A: Yes. If you are 65 years old or older and have been a legal permanent resident of the United States for 20 or more years, you may study only the questions marked with an asterisk. This reduces the number of questions you need to prepare. (Source: 100_questions_2008.txt)

## Q3: How should I answer questions about current elected or appointed officials?
A: You must provide the name of the official who is serving at the time of your eligibility interview with USCIS. Because these answers change with elections and appointments, you should verify current names at uscis.gov/citizenship/testupdates before your interview. (Source: 100_questions_2008.txt)

## Q4: Will USCIS accept correct answers that differ from the official list?
A: Although USCIS recognizes that additional correct answers may exist, applicants are encouraged to use the answers provided in the official document. The USCIS Officer will not accept an incorrect answer. (Source: 100_questions_2008.txt)

## Q5: What topics do the 100 civics questions cover?
A: The questions are divided into three main categories: American Government (principles, system of government, and rights/responsibilities), American History (colonial period to recent history), and Integrated Civics (geography, symbols, and holidays). (Source: 100_questions_2008.txt)

## Q6: How should District of Columbia residents answer questions about state Senators, Governors, or capitals?
A: D.C. residents should answer that the District has no U.S. Senators, no Governor, and is not a state and therefore does not have a capital. Residents of U.S. territories should provide the capital of their territory and note if they have no voting Representatives in Congress. (Source: 100_questions_2008.txt)
