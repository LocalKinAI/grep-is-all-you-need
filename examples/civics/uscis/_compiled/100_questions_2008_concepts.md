# 100_questions_2008.txt — Core Concepts

## Thesis
This text lists the 100 official USCIS civics questions and answers for the U.S. naturalization oral test, covering American government, history, and geography, with rules requiring current official names and reduced study options for qualifying elderly applicants.

## Core Concepts
- **Constitutional Supremacy**: The Constitution is the supreme law, establishing the federal government and protecting basic rights through 27 amendments. > "What is the supreme law of the land? . the Constitution" — A: Principles of American Democracy
- **Popular Sovereignty**: Government authority derives from the citizenry. > "The idea of self-government is in the first three words of the Constitution. What are these words? . We the People" — A: Principles of American Democracy
- **Checks and Balances**: Three separate branches limit each other’s power. > "What stops one branch of government from becoming too powerful? . checks and balances . separation of powers" — B: System of Government
- **Federalism**: Powers are divided between the federal government (e.g., print money, declare war) and states (e.g., education, police, driver’s licenses). > "Under our Constitution, some powers belong to the federal government... What is one power of the states?" — B: System of Government
- **Citizenship Rights & Responsibilities**: Citizens alone may vote in federal elections, run for federal office, and serve on juries; all residents share freedoms of speech, religion, and assembly. > "What is one responsibility that is only for United States citizens? . serve on a jury . vote in a federal election" — C: Rights and Responsibilities
- **American Historical Arc**: Key eras include colonial independence (1776), the Civil War and abolition, 20th-century world wars, and the civil rights movement. > "What did Martin Luther King, Jr. do? . fought for civil rights . worked for equality for all Americans" — C: Recent American History

## Key Quotes
> "The civics test is an oral test and the USCIS Officer will ask the applicant up to 10 of the 100 civics questions. An applicant must answer 6 out of 10 questions correctly to pass the civics portion of the naturalization test." — Introduction

> "On the naturalization test, some answers may change because of elections or appointments... Answer these questions with the name of the official who is serving at the time of your eligibility interview with USCIS." — Introduction

> "If you are 65 years old or older and have been a legal permanent resident of the United States for 20 or more years, you may study just the questions that have been marked with an asterisk." — Introduction

## Practical Points
- Applicants must provide the current serving official (President, Vice President, Governor, Senators, Representatives) at the time of their USCIS interview; outdated names are rejected.
- Applicants aged 65+ with 20+ years of legal permanent residency may study only the asterisked questions to qualify for the civics portion.
