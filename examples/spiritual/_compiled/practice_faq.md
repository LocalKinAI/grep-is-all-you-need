# The Practice of the Presence of God — FAQ

## Q1: Who was Brother Lawrence and why is his work important?
A: Nicholas Herman (Brother Lawrence) was a 17th-century Lay Brother among the Carmelites in Paris who experienced spiritual conversion at age eighteen after seeing a bare tree. His teachings on maintaining constant awareness of God's presence became influential because they demonstrated that ordinary, humble work (like his kitchen duties) could be as spiritually transformative as formal prayer.

## Q2: What is the core practice Brother Lawrence teaches?
A: The practice consists of maintaining a continual, conscious conversation with God throughout daily life—whether working, eating, or in formal prayer. Rather than separating "prayer time" from regular activities, he emphasizes doing everything "for the love of God" with constant inward attention to His presence.

## Q3: How should I handle wandering thoughts during prayer?
A: Don't be discouraged or troubled by them. Gently and calmly recall your mind to God without anxiety. Brother Lawrence recommends keeping your mind in God's presence throughout the day (not just during prayer times), which naturally makes it easier to maintain focus during formal prayer.

## Q4: Does Brother Lawrence require strict religious disciplines and mortifications?
A: No. He argues that bodily mortifications are useless unless they serve the goal of union with God through love. He emphasizes simplicity, freedom, and acting "in holy freedom" without violent constraint, focusing on intention rather than rigid external practices.

## Q5: How long does it take to develop this habitual sense of God's presence?
A: It varies. Brother Lawrence experienced ten years of struggle before achieving peace and constant joy. However, he assures that with faithful practice and God's grace, a habit will form naturally—eventually it becomes "as difficult not to think of God as it was at first to accustom myself to it."

## Q6: Can I practice this while working and managing worldly affairs?
A: Yes, absolutely. Brother Lawrence worked in the monastery kitchen and handled business affairs (buying wine, managing provisions). The practice works best in active life: "I was more united to God in my outward employments than when I left them for devotion in retirement."

## Q7: What if I'm struggling with guilt about past sins?
A: Simply confess them to God without anxiety and continue. Brother Lawrence teaches: "I shall never do otherwise, if You leave me to myself; it is You who must hinder my falling." After confession, resume your practice without discouragement or self-recrimination.
